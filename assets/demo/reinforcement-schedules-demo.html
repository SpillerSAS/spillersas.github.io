Key Takeaways for Marketers

ScheduleResponse RateExtinctionReal-World Use
Continuous Steady but moderate Very fast — customers notice immediately when rewards stop Free samples, instant cashback, onboarding incentives
Fixed Ratio Post-reinforcement pause, then burst Moderate — customers stop once they realize the pattern is broken Punch cards, "Buy 5 get 1 free," frequent-flyer tiers
Variable Ratio High and steady — "maybe this time!" Very slow — hardest to extinguish (the gambler's fallacy) Lottery promos, gacha/loot boxes, surprise discount pop-ups

Try toggling Extinction and keep clicking — notice which schedule makes you want to keep pressing even without rewards. That's the power of variable ratio reinforcement in consumer behavior.
